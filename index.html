Data Visualisation Course Project

We are Team CFLens! Welcome to the exciting world of data visualization with CFLens! In this course project, we delve into the realms of Codeforces, a premier platform for competitive programming enthusiasts. CFLens offers a unique perspective, transcending the mere lines of code to unveil the intricate patterns, trends, and insights hidden within the vast repository of Codeforces submissions. As we embark on this journey, we aim to not only see beyond the code but also to harness the power of visualization to unravel the secrets of programming competitions. Join us as we explore the realms of data, code, and visualization to gain deeper insights into the world of competitive programming like never before.

Journey of the Top Coders

See the progress of best coders from the start of codeforces till today. This chart used a horizontal bar chart to show the competition between the topmost coders on codeforces and their rating changes over the years. The horizontal axis represents the rating and different bars represent users. Right from the start Tourist from Belarus has been at the top, while Indians have struggled to get into the top standings despite being in large numbers on codeforces today.

Since we wanted to show the standings change live right from the start of the Codeforces uptil today, we chose a bar chart race as the most suitable chart which keeps on changing as time changes.

Distribution of Popularity of Codeforces over the World.

In the graph below, one can see the average and maximum rating of each country and the best institutions of that country by clicking on them. By the use of world map we see the distribution of the sport of the competitive programming on Codeforces with useful metrics like average rating and maximum ratings of countries. This gives a brief about the enthusiasm and spread of competitive programming culture over the world. The world map is made interactive with tooltips on hover to show the values. Also there is a dropdown to select the datatype to show whether average or maximum rating. We have shown the density with the help of sequential shades of blue.

With the help of this one can view and compare various top institutions of a particular country. This can be extremely useful for new college joining students to judge the competitive coding cultures of various top colleges.
